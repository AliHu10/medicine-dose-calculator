GI motility disorders, nausea/vomiting: Suspension [International product]:

Infants and Children <12 years: Efficacy has not been demonstrated; use is not recommended. Approved age range may vary by country/region; consult local product labeling.

Children ≥12 years and Adolescents: Note: Use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration necessary.

<35 kg: Oral: 0.25 mg/kg/dose up to 3 times daily (maximum: Lesser of 0.75 mg/kg/day or 30 mg/day). Use of this product in patients <35 kg is not recommended in some countries; consult local product labeling.

≥35 kg: Oral: 10 mg up to 3 times daily (maximum: 30 mg/day).

Dosing: Altered Kidney Function: Pediatric

There are no specific dosage adjustments provided in the manufacturer’s labeling; however, depending on severity of impairment, frequency should be reduced to 1 to 2 times daily and dosage reduction considered with repeated administration.

Dosing: Hepatic Impairment: Pediatric

Mild impairment: There are no dosage adjustments provided in the manufacturer’s labeling; use with caution (undergoes hepatic metabolism).

Moderate or severe impairment: Use is contraindicated.

Note: Use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration necessary.

GI motility disorders (diabetic gastroparesis, gastritis): Oral: 10 mg 3 times daily (maximum: 30 mg/day). The American College of Gastroenterology recommends initiating at 10 mg 3 times daily.

Nausea/vomiting associated with dopamine-agonist anti-Parkinson agents: Oral: 10 mg 3 times daily (maximum: 30 mg/day).

Dosage adjustment for concomitant therapy: Significant drug interactions exist, requiring dose/frequency adjustment or avoidance. Consult drug interactions database for more information.
